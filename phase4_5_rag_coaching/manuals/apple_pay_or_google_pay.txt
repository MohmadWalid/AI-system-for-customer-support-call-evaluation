Here are 5 clear rules for agents handling Apple Pay or Google Pay issues:

- **Verify Customer's Payment Method**: Before troubleshooting, verify that the customer's American Express card is properly set up in Apple Pay or Google Pay and that it is eligible for top-up transactions.

- **Check Top-up Eligibility**: Inform the customer that top-up services may not be supported on all devices or payment methods, and confirm that their device (e.g. Apple Watch) and payment method (e.g. American Express) are eligible for top-up transactions.

- **Troubleshoot Google Pay Issues**: If the customer is experiencing issues with Google Pay, ask them to check that their Google Pay account is properly linked to their bank account and that the top-up service is supported by their bank.

- **American Express Card Limitations**: If the customer is using an American Express card with Apple Pay, inform them that some American Express cards may have limitations or restrictions on top-up transactions, and offer to verify the specific card details to confirm eligibility.

- **Escalate to Technical Support if Necessary**: If the customer's issue persists after troubleshooting, offer to escalate the issue to technical support for further assistance, and provide the customer with a clear explanation of the next steps and expected resolution timeframe.

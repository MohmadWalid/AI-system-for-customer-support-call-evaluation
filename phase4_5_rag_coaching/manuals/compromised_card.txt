Here are 5 clear rules for agents handling compromised card issues:

- **Rule 1: Acknowledge and Empathize**: The agent must acknowledge the customer's concern and express empathy in their response, e.g., "I understand your concern about possible unauthorized transactions on your account, and I'm here to help."

- **Rule 2: Gather Information**: The agent must ask the customer to provide specific details about the suspected unauthorized transactions, including the date and amount of the transactions, and any other relevant information.

- **Rule 3: Flag Account for Fraud**: The agent must immediately flag the customer's account for potential fraud and take steps to prevent further unauthorized transactions, such as blocking the card or placing a hold on the account.

- **Rule 4: Provide Next Steps**: The agent must clearly explain the next steps to the customer, including:
	* Issuing a replacement card
	* Reimbursing the customer for unauthorized transactions (if applicable)
	* Providing additional security measures to prevent future incidents
	* Offering to monitor the account for suspicious activity

- **Rule 5: Document and Escalate**: The agent must document the customer's concern and the actions taken in the customer's account records, and escalate the issue to a supervisor or specialized fraud team if necessary, to ensure a thorough investigation and resolution.
